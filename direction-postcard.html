From the Editor —

Open the door.
The bread is that good.

For nineteen years, the same small storefront in the Henry Frank Arcade has been baking the same Dutch Crunch loaf at the same hour, and the same line has been forming around the corner just before lunch. The line is not a problem. The line is the point. You stand in it because at the end of it is a sandwich named after Frank Sinatra or Bob Hope or — depending on what you can stomach naming out loud — somebody else, and because nothing about the place is trying to be anywhere else.

It is open daily, 10 to 6, and most days you get a lollipop on the way out. Twenty-four specialties at $14.75. A tomato-red Bomb Sauce that Yelp writes love letters to. A house-made sun-tea pitcher on the counter. Twenty-four-fifty-seven five-star reviews and counting. The mountains are still there.

"Worth the wait. Worth the line. Worth the entire afternoon."
— Yelp · 4.7★ · 2,457 reviews and counting

We are not, technically, the only sandwich shop in Palm Springs. We are simply the one ranked #4 of 345 restaurants on TripAdvisor, and the one whose Dutch Crunch is the only thing locals will agree on. That is enough.

A Field Guide —

The bread, in cross-section.

Dutch Crunch — the Northern California staple — gets its name from the cracked tortoise-shell crust formed by a rice-flour paste applied just before baking. Ours is mixed at sunrise, baked by 8am, and at any given moment the loaf you're holding is no more than a few hours old. It is the only sandwich-bread in Palm Springs that requires both hands and a paper bag.

Crust shatters on contact. Crumb is roughly thirty percent air. Tomato slides slightly. This is correct. This is the design.

Sidebar · Section II

The Palm Springs Hall of Fame.

A short field guide for tourists. So you know which sandwich you're eating.

"The Chairman"

Frank Sinatra

Wintered in Rancho Mirage. Drank martinis at Melvyn's. The first sandwich on the menu, and rightfully so.

"Thanks for the Memories"

Bob Hope

Built a UFO-shaped house in the hills you can still see from the freeway. Hosted the desert classic golf for forty years.

"Copacabana"

Barry Manilow

Lives here. Yes, still. Yes, the song. Yes, hot.

"The Dinah"

Dinah Shore

The Dinah Shore Classic at Mission Hills made Palm Springs the unofficial capital of women's golf — and gay weekend.

"Sunny and Share"

Sonny & Cher

Got married in Tijuana. Performed at the Riviera. Sandwich is BLT-and-avocado, which is to say honest.

"The Thighmaster"

Suzanne Somers

Lived in Palm Springs. Sold the device on infomercials. The sandwich does not, regrettably, tone anything.

"The Federer / The Djokovic / The Rafa"

BNP Paribas Open

The fifth Grand Slam, played at Indian Wells every March. The line on tournament Saturday is, frankly, a religious experience.

"The Gene Autry"

Gene Autry

Owned the Palm Springs Angels. Sang to horses. Has a hotel named after him on Indian Canyon.

"But It's a Dry Heat"

The Locals

A regional joke about 117° afternoons. Nobody believes it. It is still spicier than the weather.
